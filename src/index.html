Why Water Quality Matters

Water is the lifeblood of our planet, essential for drinking, agriculture, industry, and countless other vital processes. Yet, the quality of our water sources is constantly under threat from pollution, climate change, and human activities. Monitoring water quality is not just a responsibility; it's an imperative.

What exactly is a water
quality problem?

Significant water quality issues include pathogenic pollution, oxygen-depleting organic pollution, salinity, and toxicity. We monitor eutrophication, oxygen depletion, and ecological health of surface water. For groundwater, we monitor fluoride, nitrate, arsenic, iron, and seawater intrusion.
